Welcome to the coding challenge portion of the interview. Below is a problem we would like for you to solve using the latest version of Angular. Feel free to make assumptions as needed, but do list them in a Readme. We would like you to complete the problem using Test Driven Development ensuring there is high test coverage.

Hello! Our team area is too quiet. We’d like to listen to some music. Each team member has selected their favorite tracks and are providing them to you in priority order. We have two half hour sections of the day to fill with music. Not all team members are present for both thirty minute sections and we do not want to play their music if they aren’t in the team area. We also do not want songs to be playing across the section boundaries. Having a period of time at the end of a section where no music is playing is acceptable, but we would prefer hearing another song if possible. Outside of each 30 minute section, our team area needs to be quiet.

  • Songs should be played in priority order when possible.
  • There is no priority or order between the team members.
  • Play a song from each person before another song from the same person.
  • Songs should not be scheduled if they will not finish playing within the 30 minute time boundary.
  • Do not play songs from people who are not present during that time section.
  • Complete playing all songs of all present people before repeating songs.
  • Input will be song name and artist, duration (M:SS), and contain the name of the person who added the song.
  • You are provided with input data which should be submitted through a form in the browser of an Angular application
  • The output should be displayed in the browser of the Angular application
  • We are looking for clean, easy to maintain, production quality code. A simple solution that runs properly is preferred over a complicated gold plated solution.
